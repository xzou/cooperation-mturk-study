Instructions

You will start the game with 100 points. These points come from your base pay of $1.00. You will get to keep at least 100 points throughout the game.

You will be playing a game with your opponent in which your goal is to collect as many points as possible over a series of rounds. On each round, you will be asked to independently make a decision to either cooperate or to defect.

If you choose to cooperate, you will pay 20 of your own points to give 40 points to your opponent. If your opponent independently also chooses to cooperate with you, you will receive 40 points.

If you choose to defect, you will pay 0 points and give nothing to your opponent. If your opponent likewise independently chooses to defect, you will receive 0 points on that round.

If you choose to cooperate with your opponent but your opponent chooses to defect, then you will pay 20 points and they will receive 40 points, but they will pay nothing and you will receive nothing.

On the contrary, if you choose to defect against your opponent but they choose to cooperate with you, then you will pay 0 points but will receive 40 points from your opponent.

Because you will be making decisions independently, you will not find out what decision your opponent made until after you submit your decision. You will get the results of each round after you click "submit."

Bonus Payment

At the end of the experiment, the number of points you have over 100 will correspond to your bonus payment, so you will need to collect as many points as possible to maximize your payout.

NOTE: you must finish the experiment to receive this bonus payment.

Opponent Predictions

You will also be asked to make predictions about your opponent throughout the game. For example, if you were playing against an opponent named Kai, you would be asked to provide your guess about the probability that Kai will cooperate with you.

Remember: a low probability (e.g. 0.1) means you think that Kai will not cooperate with you. A high probability (e.g. 0.9) means you are certain Kai will cooperate with you. Use the scale provided to be as accurate as possible about your predictions.

Click "Next" to proceed to the quiz. You must answer correctly to continue with the experiment.
